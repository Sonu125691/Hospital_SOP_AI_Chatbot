# Blood Bank Operations

## SOP Information
Department: Blood Bank Operations
SOP Code: BB-SOP-001
Version: 1.0
Effective Date: 01-Apr-2026

## Purpose 
To define standardized procedures for the safe collection, testing, storage, and transfusion of blood and blood components in order to ensure patient safety and regulatory compliance.

## Scope  
This SOP applies to all personnel involved in blood bank operations, including Blood Bank Officers, laboratory technicians, nursing staff, and duty doctors. It covers donor management, blood processing, storage, issue, and transfusion support.

## Procedure  

### Donor Registration and Screening
1. Register donor details in the blood bank system.  
2. Verify donor identity and consent.  
3. Conduct medical history assessment and physical examination.  
4. Perform hemoglobin and basic health checks.  
5. Approve or defer donor based on eligibility criteria.

### Blood Collection
1. Prepare sterile blood collection equipment.  
2. Label blood bags with unique identification numbers.  
3. Collect blood under aseptic conditions.  
4. Monitor donor during and after collection.  
5. Record collection details in the blood bank register.

### Testing and Processing
1. Perform blood grouping (ABO and Rh).  
2. Conduct mandatory infectious disease screening.  
3. Separate whole blood into components if required.  
4. Update test results in the laboratory system.  
5. Quarantine blood units until all tests are cleared.

### Storage
1. Store whole blood at 2–6°C in designated refrigerators.  
2. Store plasma and platelets under recommended conditions.  
3. Monitor storage temperatures continuously.  
4. Maintain FIFO (First-In, First-Out) inventory control.  
5. Discard expired or contaminated units as per protocol.

### Issue and Transfusion Support
1. Receive blood requisition form from treating physician.  
2. Verify patient details and blood group compatibility.  
3. Perform cross-matching.  
4. Issue blood unit after approval from Blood Bank Officer.  
5. Record issuance details and handover to nursing staff.  
6. Support transfusion process and monitor for reactions.

## Responsibilities

### Blood Bank Officer
- Overall supervision of blood bank operations  
- Approval of blood issue and donor eligibility  
- Ensuring regulatory compliance  

### Laboratory Technician
- Blood testing and processing  
- Equipment maintenance  
- Data entry and record keeping  

### Nursing Staff 
- Assisting in blood collection and transfusion  
- Monitoring donors and patients  
- Reporting adverse reactions  

### Duty Doctor
- Clinical approval for transfusions  
- Management of transfusion reactions  
- Coordination with blood bank team  

## Documentation

- Donor Registration Form (BB-01)  
- Donor Consent Form (BB-02)  
- Blood Collection Register (BB-03)  
- Blood Testing Record Sheet (BB-04)  
- Blood Requisition Form (BB-05)  
- Blood Issue Log (BB-06)  
- Transfusion Reaction Report Form (BB-07)  

## Review Cycle  
This SOP shall be reviewed every 12 months or earlier if required due to regulatory changes, audit findings, or operational updates.

## Approval Authority  

- Medical Superintendent  
- Head of Pathology Department  
- Blood Bank Officer In-Charge  



# Infection Control Protocol  

## SOP Information
Department: Infection Control Protocol
SOP Code: IC-SOP-002  
Version: 1.0  
Effective Date: 01-Apr-2026  

## Purpose  
To establish standardized infection prevention and control measures in order to minimize the risk of healthcare-associated infections (HAIs) and ensure a safe environment for patients, staff, and visitors.

## Scope  
This SOP applies to all hospital personnel, including doctors, nurses, laboratory staff, housekeeping staff, and administrative staff. It covers hand hygiene, use of personal protective equipment (PPE), environmental cleaning, isolation precautions, and biomedical waste handling.

## Procedure  

### Hand Hygiene
1. Perform hand hygiene before and after every patient contact.  
2. Use alcohol-based hand rubs or soap and water as appropriate.  
3. Wash hands for at least 20 seconds when visibly soiled.  
4. Follow the WHO 5 Moments for Hand Hygiene.  
5. Dry hands using disposable paper towels or air dryers.

### Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)
1. Wear appropriate PPE based on the level of infection risk.  
2. Don gloves, masks, gowns, and face shields as required.  
3. Change PPE between patients and procedures.  
4. Dispose of used PPE in designated biomedical waste bins.  
5. Perform hand hygiene after removing PPE.

### Isolation Precautions
1. Identify patients requiring isolation based on clinical assessment.  
2. Place patients in designated isolation rooms when necessary.  
3. Display appropriate isolation signage outside patient rooms.  
4. Restrict visitor access as per infection control guidelines.  
5. Use dedicated equipment for isolated patients.

### Environmental Cleaning and Disinfection
1. Clean and disinfect patient care areas daily.  
2. Use hospital-approved disinfectants.  
3. Focus on high-touch surfaces such as bed rails, door handles, and switches.  
4. Follow terminal cleaning procedures after patient discharge.  
5. Maintain cleaning logs and schedules.

### Surveillance and Reporting
1. Monitor and record healthcare-associated infections (HAIs).  
2. Report infection incidents to the Infection Control Committee.  
3. Conduct root cause analysis for infection outbreaks.  
4. Implement corrective and preventive actions.  
5. Review infection trends periodically.

## Responsibilities  

### Infection Control Officer 
- Overall supervision of infection control activities  
- Policy implementation and compliance monitoring  
- Reporting to hospital management  

### Nursing Staff
- Adherence to hand hygiene and PPE protocols  
- Monitoring patient isolation practices  
- Reporting suspected infections  

### Housekeeping Staff
- Environmental cleaning and disinfection  
- Waste segregation and disposal  
- Maintenance of cleaning records  

### Medical Staff
- Clinical assessment of infection risks  
- Prescribing isolation precautions  
- Reporting infection-related incidents  

## Documentation  

- Hand Hygiene Audit Form (IC-01)  
- PPE Usage Log (IC-02)  
- Isolation Register (IC-03)  
- Environmental Cleaning Checklist (IC-04)  
- Infection Surveillance Report (IC-05)  
- Incident Reporting Form (IC-06)  

## Review Cycle  
This SOP shall be reviewed every 12 months or earlier if required due to regulatory updates, audit findings, or changes in infection control guidelines.

# Biomedical Waste Management  

## SOP Information
Department: Biomedical Waste Management 
SOP Code: BMW-SOP-003  
Version: 1.0  
Effective Date: 01-Apr-2026  

## Purpose  
To define standardized procedures for the segregation, collection, storage, transportation, and disposal of biomedical waste in order to ensure environmental safety, regulatory compliance, and protection of healthcare workers and the public.

## Scope  
This SOP applies to all hospital personnel, including doctors, nurses, laboratory staff, housekeeping staff, and waste management personnel. It covers all categories of biomedical waste generated in clinical, laboratory, and support service areas.

## Procedure  

### Waste Segregation
1. Segregate biomedical waste at the point of generation.  
2. Use color-coded bins as per biomedical waste management rules.  
3. Ensure non-mixing of biomedical and general waste.  
4. Label waste containers clearly with waste category and date.  
5. Train staff on correct segregation practices.

### Waste Collection
1. Collect biomedical waste at scheduled intervals.  
2. Use designated trolleys for waste transportation.  
3. Ensure waste bags are properly sealed before collection.  
4. Avoid manual handling of waste bags where possible.  
5. Record waste collection details in the waste logbook.

### Temporary Storage
1. Store biomedical waste in a designated waste storage area.  
2. Restrict access to authorized personnel only.  
3. Ensure storage area is well-ventilated and secured.  
4. Do not store waste beyond the permissible time limit.  
5. Maintain cleanliness of the storage area.

### Transportation and Disposal
1. Transport waste to the disposal facility using covered vehicles.  
2. Hand over waste to authorized biomedical waste treatment agencies.  
3. Ensure proper documentation during waste handover.  
4. Maintain records of waste disposal certificates.  
5. Monitor compliance with disposal schedules.

### Spill Management
1. Isolate the area in case of a waste spill.  
2. Wear appropriate PPE before handling spills.  
3. Clean the spill using approved disinfectants.  
4. Dispose of contaminated materials as biomedical waste.  
5. Report spill incidents to the Waste Management Officer.

# Fire Safety and Emergency Evacuation  

## SOP Information
Department: Fire Safety and Emergency Evacuation
SOP Code: FS-SOP-004  
Version: 1.0  
Effective Date: 01-Apr-2026  

## Purpose  
To establish standardized procedures for fire prevention, early detection, emergency response, and safe evacuation in order to protect patients, staff, visitors, and hospital property from fire-related hazards.

## Scope  
This SOP applies to all hospital personnel, including doctors, nurses, administrative staff, housekeeping staff, security staff, and visitors. It covers fire prevention measures, response to fire incidents, evacuation protocols, and coordination with emergency services.

## Procedure  

### Fire Prevention
1. Ensure all electrical equipment is properly maintained and inspected.  
2. Avoid overloading electrical sockets and extension cords.  
3. Store flammable materials in designated areas.  
4. Maintain clear access to fire exits and corridors.  
5. Conduct regular fire safety audits and inspections.

### Fire Detection and Alarm
1. Activate the nearest fire alarm upon detection of fire or smoke.  
2. Inform the hospital fire control room immediately.  
3. Use public address systems to announce emergency alerts if required.  
4. Identify the location and type of fire.  
5. Do not attempt to silence alarms without authorization.

### Initial Fire Response
1. Use appropriate fire extinguishers for small, controllable fires.  
2. Follow the PASS technique (Pull, Aim, Squeeze, Sweep).  
3. Do not attempt to fight large or rapidly spreading fires.  
4. Close doors and windows to contain the fire if safe to do so.  
5. Evacuate the area immediately if the fire cannot be controlled.

### Evacuation Procedure
1. Assist patients and visitors to evacuate calmly.  
2. Prioritize evacuation of critical and immobile patients.  
3. Use designated emergency exits and stairways only.  
4. Do not use elevators during a fire emergency.  
5. Assemble at the designated safe assembly point.  
6. Conduct headcounts to ensure all persons are evacuated.

### Coordination with Emergency Services
1. Inform the local fire department immediately.  
2. Provide details of the fire location and severity.  
3. Assign a liaison officer to coordinate with firefighters.  
4. Facilitate access routes for fire engines.  
5. Support emergency response operations as required.

## Purpose  
To define standardized procedures for the timely, appropriate, and safe admission of patients to the Intensive Care Unit (ICU) in order to ensure optimal utilization of ICU resources and delivery of critical care services.

## Scope  
This SOP applies to all hospital personnel involved in ICU admissions, including emergency physicians, intensivists, duty doctors, nursing staff, and ICU coordinators. It covers patient triage, admission criteria, approval workflow, bed allocation, and transfer procedures.

## Procedure  

### Patient Identification and Referral
1. Identify patients requiring intensive care based on clinical assessment.  
2. Refer patients from Emergency Department, wards, or operating theatre.  
3. Document clinical indications for ICU admission.  
4. Initiate ICU admission request in the hospital system.  
5. Notify the ICU coordinator of the referral.

### Triage and Admission Criteria 
1. Assess patient severity using standardized scoring systems (e.g., NEWS, SOFA).  
2. Prioritize patients based on urgency and survival benefit.  
3. Confirm availability of ICU beds and required equipment.  
4. Review contraindications for ICU admission if any.  
5. Document triage decision and priority status.

### Approval and Bed Allocation
1. Obtain approval from the intensivist or duty consultant.  
2. Assign ICU bed based on patient condition and infection status.  
3. Reserve necessary equipment (ventilator, monitors, infusion pumps).  
4. Inform nursing staff of incoming patient.  
5. Update ICU bed management system.

### Patient Transfer to ICU
1. Stabilize patient before transfer.  
2. Ensure continuous monitoring during transport.  
3. Transfer patient with appropriate medical escort.  
4. Carry complete medical records and investigation reports.  
5. Hand over patient to ICU nursing team upon arrival.

### Admission Documentation  
1. Complete ICU Admission Form.  
2. Record baseline vitals and clinical parameters.  
3. Obtain informed consent from patient or next of kin.  
4. Update electronic medical records.  
5. Initiate ICU care plan and treatment orders.

## Purpose  
To define standardized procedures for the efficient, timely, and safe management of patients presenting to the Emergency Department (ED) in order to ensure rapid assessment, stabilization, and appropriate clinical intervention.

## Scope  
This SOP applies to all personnel involved in emergency services, including emergency physicians, nursing staff, paramedics, triage officers, and support staff. It covers patient intake, triage, emergency treatment, referrals, and coordination with other hospital departments.

## Procedure  

### Patient Reception and Registration
1. Receive patients at the emergency reception area.  
2. Register patient details in the hospital information system.  
3. Assign a unique emergency identification number.  
4. Record presenting complaints and arrival time.  
5. Direct patients to the triage area immediately.

### Triage and Prioritization
1. Assess patients using standardized triage protocols (e.g., ESI, CTAS).  
2. Categorize patients based on severity and urgency.  
3. Prioritize life-threatening conditions for immediate care.  
4. Document triage category and time of assessment.  
5. Reassess patients periodically while waiting.

### Emergency Assessment and Stabilization
1. Conduct primary survey (Airway, Breathing, Circulation, Disability, Exposure).  
2. Initiate resuscitation measures as required.  
3. Administer emergency medications.  
4. Perform essential investigations (ECG, X-ray, blood tests).  
5. Monitor vital signs continuously.

### Emergency Treatment and Procedures
1. Provide definitive emergency treatment as per clinical protocols.  
2. Perform minor procedures in the emergency procedure room.  
3. Arrange specialist consultations if required.  
4. Prepare patients for emergency surgery if indicated.  
5. Document all treatments and procedures performed.

### Referral, Admission, or Discharge
1. Decide patient disposition based on clinical condition.  
2. Admit patients to wards or ICU as required.  
3. Refer patients to other departments or external facilities if needed.  
4. Discharge patients with appropriate instructions and prescriptions.  
5. Update patient records and close emergency case file.

### Mass Casualty and Disaster Management
1. Activate disaster management protocol during mass casualty events.  
2. Establish triage zones for disaster victims.  
3. Mobilize additional staff and resources.

## Purpose  
To define standardized procedures for the safe, sterile, and efficient functioning of the Operation Theatre (OT) in order to ensure patient safety, surgical quality, and compliance with infection control and clinical standards.

## Scope  
This SOP applies to all personnel involved in surgical services, including surgeons, anesthesiologists, nursing staff, OT technicians, housekeeping staff, and support staff. It covers pre-operative preparation, intra-operative procedures, post-operative protocols, and OT sterilization practices.

## Procedure  

### Pre-Operative Preparation
1. Verify patient identity, consent, and surgical site.  
2. Review surgical checklist and pre-operative investigations.  
3. Ensure availability of required instruments and implants.  
4. Prepare OT with sterile equipment and supplies.  
5. Conduct pre-operative team briefing (time-out procedure).

### Patient Transfer to OT
1. Transfer patient using appropriate transport equipment.  
2. Ensure patient is accompanied by medical staff.  
3. Verify patient details at OT reception.  
4. Maintain patient privacy and dignity.  
5. Handover patient to OT nursing team.

### Intra-Operative Procedures
1. Maintain strict aseptic technique throughout surgery.  
2. Monitor patient vitals continuously.  
3. Follow surgical safety checklist.  
4. Document surgical events and implant details.  
5. Count surgical instruments and sponges before closure.

### Anesthesia Management
1. Conduct pre-anesthesia evaluation.  
2. Administer anesthesia as per protocol.  
3. Monitor airway, breathing, and circulation.  
4. Record anesthesia details and medications used.  
5. Manage intra-operative complications.

### Post-Operative Care and Recovery
1. Transfer patient to recovery room post-surgery.  
2. Monitor vital signs and pain levels.  
3. Manage post-operative medications and fluids.  
4. Observe for surgical complications.  
5. Document recovery progress and handover to ward staff.

### OT Cleaning and Sterilization
1. Clean OT surfaces after each procedure.  
2. Sterilize surgical instruments using approved methods.  
3. Dispose of biomedical waste as per protocol.
